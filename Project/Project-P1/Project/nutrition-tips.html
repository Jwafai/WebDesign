2-POST-WORKOUT MEALS

Post-workout meals are the most important to consume after you work out. These include protein, carbs and fruit. The best time to eat protein is with in an hour after you work out because your muscles are hungry for nutrients. Post-workout meals are essential to gain muscle. They help you build your muscles by giving them the nutrients they need.

3-MEALS SCHDUELING

scheduling what and when you eat will help you maintain a balanced diet and create a more stable energy source, as your metabolism will be engaged at optimal levels all day long. The goal is to eat every 1 to 4 hours in order to keep your blood sugar consistent, for your stomach to optimally digest, to build muscle, and to fully recover

4-FOODS TO AVOID

It’s not just what you should eat, but what you shouldn’t. Stay away from processed foods and sugary drinks. If you’re going to drink, stick with water or unsweetened tea. Soda is loaded with sugar and calories that will make it harder for you to lose weight or body shape. When your body is dehydrated, it will hold onto water weight and you’ll feel bloated.

5-DRINKING WATER

When you exercise, your body sweats as it tries to return to its optimal temperature. As sweat evaporates from your skin, it removes heat from the body, but you also lose body fluid. You need to drink fluid during exercise to replace the fluids you lose when you sweat. That way, you’ll reduce the risk of heat stress, maintain normal body function, and maintain performance levels.Drink 3-5 Liters per day
